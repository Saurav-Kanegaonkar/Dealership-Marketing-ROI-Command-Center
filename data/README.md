# Data Notes

All data in this repository is synthetic and generated by `scripts/score_operating_data.py`.

The generator models a two-rooftop automotive dealer group with Ford, Chrysler Dodge Jeep Ram, commercial, service, and mobile-service-style operations. It does not contain real customer, VIN, CRM, DMS, advertising, phone, website, or repair order data.

## Modeled source structure

- `customers.csv` models owner and vehicle-level retention signals.
- `campaign_performance.csv` models weekly spend, appointments, repair orders, sales units, and attributed gross.
- `service_opportunities.csv` models customer-level campaign triggers and priority scores.
- `source_quality_checks.csv` models data freshness, match quality, join keys, and controls.
- `ai_use_cases.csv` models practical AI experiments with effort, expected gross, risk, and control fields.

## Synthetic assumptions

- Two rooftops are weighted roughly 57 percent Chrysler Dodge Jeep Ram and 43 percent Ford.
- Customer records include retail, commercial, and fleet segments.
- Service opportunities are triggered by open recalls, state inspection timing, service lapse, declined work, Hunter alignment findings, and commercial fleet service patterns.
- Campaign channels include email, SMS, phone, paid search, paid social, recall outreach, declined service recovery, commercial prospecting, and third-party vendor leads.
- Attributed gross is modeled from booked appointments, show rates, repair-order gross, and a small number of vehicle sales for inventory or lead-provider campaigns.
- Quality risk is a transparent score based on source freshness, record match rate, join key quality, and whether the source needs a clear owner.
